Everything you need to know about Precautionary/ Booster Dose

India started the precautionary dose, also known as the Booster Dose, when coronavirus infections in the country were once again on a steep upward trajectory. The Precautionary Dose is the third dose of vaccine given to people with compromised immune systems despite receiving the first two doses. Its goal is to strengthen the magnitude of protection by producing more antibodies. As of February 2022, the precautionary dose is for healthcare workers, frontline workers and senior citizens. There is no need for new registration for those eligible for the Precautionary Dose or the third dose of covid vaccine. Also, there no mixing of vaccines shall be done for the Booster Dose. Those who received two doses of Covishield (manufactured by Serum Institute of India) will receive the same vaccine as the third dose. The same goes for Covaxin (manufactured by Bharat Biotech International Ltd) and Sputnik.

Download Your Covid Vaccination Certificate

A Covid Vaccine Certificate is a document issued by the government as an assurance to the beneficiary on the type of vaccination along with the number of doses. For individuals who have taken just the first dose of the vaccine, the provisional Covid Vaccination Certificate also provides the next vaccination date. The certificate is an evidence for the beneficiary to have access to public places and in case of travel.
